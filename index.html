THE COBWEB

People usually think that if it’s on the Web it will stay on the Web, so they would use the Web to archive their memories. While the Web serves as infinite storage for information, nothing changes more rapidly than the Web. I frequently use a Korean online community, similar to Reddit, to find useful and interesting information. And whenever I see something interesting, I would keep the tabs open. So on my Chrome browser, there would be 5~10 websites opened, waiting to be seen. But sometimes, after a few days, I notice that there’s a grey circle on the right side of the tab, and when I click it, it shows an alert saying We can’t find this post ......
As I kept seeing this alert, it got me thinking wouldn’t it be paradoxical to expect something on the fluid and ever-changing Web to last forever? I mean, could anything last forever? What if Internet Archive and the Wayback Machine disappears? What if the Web disappears? In the end, everything goes.

The internet didn’t kill counterculture ...

The Web feels free, so it feels like anyone’s open to express anything that would like through social media. But even if they express different things, I feel like what they essentially want is attention. People on social media promote themselves, life, uniqueness, but how is it different from others? This almost feels pretentious. People become increasingly conscious of other people’s eyes and shape themselves to get more attention. Now even the radicalness or craziness of oneself is a common feature among people on social media. You think you’re different, but actually, you’re just one of them. So like it was mentioned to be truly countercultural today, “one has to betray the platform, which may come in the form of betraying or divesting from your public online self.”
