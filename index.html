THE COBWEB

People usually think that if it’s on the Web it will stay on the Web, so they would use the Web to archive their memories. While the Web serves as infinite storage for information, nothing changes more rapidly than the Web. I frequently use a Korean online community, similar to Reddit, to find useful and interesting information. And whenever I see something interesting, I would keep the tabs open. So on my Chrome browser, there would be 5~10 websites opened, waiting to be seen. But sometimes, after a few days, I notice that there’s a grey circle on the right side of the tab, and when I click it, it shows an alert saying We can’t find this post ......
As I kept seeing this alert, it got me thinking wouldn’t it be paradoxical to expect something on the fluid and ever-changing Web to last forever? I mean, could anything last forever? What if Internet Archive and the Wayback Machine disappears? What if the Web disappears? In the end, everything goes.

The internet didn’t kill counterculture ...

The Web feels free, so it feels like anyone’s open to express anything that would like through social media. But even if they express different things, I feel like what they essentially want is attention. People on social media promote themselves, life, uniqueness, but how is it different from others? This almost feels pretentious. People become increasingly conscious of other people’s eyes and shape themselves to get more attention. Now even the radicalness or craziness of oneself is a common feature among people on social media. You think you’re different, but actually, you’re just one of them. So like it was mentioned to be truly countercultural today, “one has to betray the platform, which may come in the form of betraying or divesting from your public online self.”

The Good Room

“If we want something, we can make space for it”
This is what I love about the web. If you want something, you can make it. And anybody can enjoy and be part of the experience. But while we can create anything, it's hard to share it with a larger audience. So for the website I create, it would probably only be visited by students from this class. So while "resonance and significance" all sounds good, it also makes me conflicted. Why create when no one's going to know? Is it going to be meaningful as a website if no one's going to visit again or stay on the website for some time? How is it different from having it on your hard drive? … I think it's just hard to create a purposeful and meaningful website that gets acknowledged.

The User Condition: ...

I’ve been feeling stupid lately. I don’t know exactly how it works, but somehow it does. Certainly, technological advances have made our lives more convenient, but it also seems to make us more and more stupid. People just accept it as it is, believing that intelligent people would have created it well. So I always listen to music on YouTube, but yet I still don’t know how autoplay works. Sometimes they play the same music again, only ploaded by a different user, and sometimes it’s utterly unrelated to what I was just listening to. I’m lazy to create a playlist (what I want to listen always changes), and I always play YouTube in the background and do something else, so it’s hard for me to search and play the music every time. So sadly, I have no choice but to rely on autoplay even though it drives me crazy from time to time…
People say that technology should continue to develop to pursue more convenience and that old fashion is not good. But is that always right? I feel like it has moved way beyond comfort and is instead becoming inconvenient and harmful in some ways. Then maybe it isn’t that absurd to imagine that robots would soon control us.

03/26/2021 17:30

CONCEPT DESIGN GAMES BOOK TWO: PLAYING

It’s interesting to see how the author explains the design process and understanding through games. Of the three concepts, what interested me the most is control. From a reading I read before, it said that every game and design work are the results of a collaborative process between designer and player/user. So if a designer constructs the building block, it is the player who completes the design. A designer cannot create on his/her own. So control goes both ways. Designers will have preliminary control of the project, and the players will have control of how they will interact with that project. I think this is a vital aspect to consider when creating my own websites, how users will react and interact, and how they could contribute to the website.

Data Sweat

What I feel when I’m online: Fuckingly annoyed by advertisements.
Sometimes I don’t know if I opened a website to see the contents or the advertisements. What’s creepy about this is when an ad about what I searched just before pops up. I don’t know when I agreed to such terms, but at some point, I must have agreed to provide my information. And because of that “agreement” these personalised advertisements appear in front of me. Although this personalised information would only appear on my computer and on the browser logged in with my account, it’s still very creepy. It feels as if my thoughts and choices are being controlled. So rather than making my life more convenient, it feels like google (or any other) is determining my interest that I don’t even know yet.

Spatial Interfaces

It would be really fun if we could have games for everything. But still, it’s hard to imagine how the game or spatial experience would look like. I imagined faces floating around in a 3d space or table in a Zoom meeting, and it was just weird and awkward. Would the spatial experience be more intuitive? I personally like the simplicity that one can experience online. For example, on Zoom, you don’t need to move your head around to see who’s talking. Every attendee is on one screen so that you can see everyone all at once. I also find spatial experience awkward because it feels weird to see something spatial on a 2d screen for me. Maybe this is because we aren’t used to those technologies? But personally, I like and feel comfortable with the 2d space, so I don’t feel the necessity to incorporate the spatial experience.

Spatial Interfaces

An app is tiny. So, it’s just amazing how a small app can do so many things. These days, it feels like anything can be done with an app. You can turn off the lights at your home, open your car, check your health, and so many other things with an app on your phone. However, while it may be convenient, it’s also somewhat scary. It may sound paradoxical, but although I use technology every day, I don’t really like it because it takes away what I can easily do in life. It makes me feel stupid using the app to do the most basic and easiest thing. If I built the app myself, I would have felt a sense of accomplishment. However, using apps that other people makes me dull. So even though I’ll continue to use apps, I still want to turn off the lights, open and drive the car, and do other things myself.
